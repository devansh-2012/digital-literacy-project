# Case Study: UPI "Request Money" Fraud
**Type of Cybercrime:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud.

**The Scenario:**
A student lists a used textbook for sale on a social media marketplace. A "buyer" contacts them immediately, claiming to be interested.
To "pay" the student, the attacker sends a "Request Money" notification through a UPI app (like PhonePe or Google Pay)[cite: 79].

**Step-by-Step Execution:**
1. **Trust Building:** The attacker uses a professional-looking profile and acts in a hurry to complete the deal.
2. **The Hook:** The attacker tells the student, "I have sent the payment link; just click it and enter your PIN to receive the money in your bank account".
3. **The Trap:** The student, unaware that a PIN is only required to *send* money, enters their 4 or 6-digit UPI PIN.
4. **The Theft:** Instead of receiving funds, the requested amount is instantly debited from the student’s linked bank account.

**Target Group:**
This crime primarily targets college students and young adults who frequently use digital payment apps for small peer-to-peer transactions.

**Consequences:**
The immediate consequence is financial loss. [cite_start]Secondary effects include psychological stress and a loss of trust in digital financial systems.
